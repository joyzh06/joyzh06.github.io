About Me

I believe that video games are the epitome of problem solving, both in the playing and making of.

I have always loved video games, and it is the reason I studied computer science. After graduating, I worked as a general software engineer. I focused on applying game design and development ideology, taking the components that make a game fun and applying them elsewhere elsewhere and taking advantage of the logic and skills naturally used when gaming.

Three years later, I finally made the leap to games. I love programming for video games, from the frustration of through the problems. I like the immediate feedback of writing something then trying it on screen, incorporating feedback, and the interdisciplinary work. I am especially interested in gameplay programming.
